Class 2: Premature Ventricular Contraction (PVC)

Premature ventricular contractions (PVCs) are extra heartbeats that begin in the ventricles. They disrupt the regular heart rhythm, sometimes causing a fluttering or skipped beat sensation.

  • Common and often harmless in healthy individuals
  • May be triggered by stress, caffeine, or certain medications
  • Frequent PVCs may indicate underlying heart disease

If PVCs are frequent or associated with symptoms (dizziness, chest pain), medical evaluation is recommended.
